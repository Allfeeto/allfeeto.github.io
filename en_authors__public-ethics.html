Publication ethics.
Memo to authors!
Ethical standards in relation to publications are needed to guarantee the high quality of scientific publications, trust in scientific research from the outside societies and recognition for scientists for their ideas.

It is necessary to avoid:

  • Fabrications and falsifications of data: data fabrication means that the researcher did not do any work, but invented the data. Falsification of data means that the researcher performed an experiment, but then changed some data. Both of these actions undermine public confidence in scientists.

  • Plagiarism: using other people's ideas and works without giving them their due is unfair and unfair. Copying at least one sentence from another manuscript or even your own from a previously published manuscript without a quotation is considered plagiarism – convey the meaning in your own words.

  • Submitting an article to several journals at the same time: it is unethical to submit one manuscript to more than one journal at the same time. Such actions take up the time of editors and reviewers, and can also damage the reputation of journals if the manuscript is published in more than one of them.

  • Duplicate publications: means the publication of similar manuscripts based on the same experiment. This will lead to the fact that readers will not pay attention to attention to your work.

Useful:

  • Quote the scientists of the scientific school whose ideas you are developing.

  • Use up-to-date material.
